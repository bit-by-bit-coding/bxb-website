Hello World Leadership Information

Overview

Hello World is now searching for the next generation of managers! We are looking for dedicated volunteers to lead and run our summer camp program Hello World which is dedicated to teach web development to our underrepresented youth. The program aims to both excite and enlighten students who are not normally exposed to actual code. If you are not familiar with the program, please read about it here. Explore our website to gain a sense of our organizationâ€™s mission and goal. Part of our mission is to provide leadership opportunities. The manager and assistant manager will have the unique opportunity to take the reins of a program that serves around a hundred of students and 20+ volunteers. Do not be intimidated if you have never directed a program before. We especially welcome high schoolers and college students to apply.

Current Openings:

Hello World Manager:

Our Hello World Manager is in charge of leading and setting the tone and direction of our program. To lead Hello World, it takes a dedication, flexability, and communication skills. It takes someone who is willing and able to manage students, parents, and volunteer effectively to run a program which teaches middle school girls and non-binary youth how to code.

The manager will be in charge of all interactions between parents, students, and Hello World. This involves student signing-ups, answering questions, solving problems, and any other necessary communication. The manager is also responsible for organizing volunteers. This includes interviewing, onboarding, leading meetings, and creating a welcoming and supportive environment.

The manager is the bridge between Hello World and all BxB Central. They will attend manager meetings and interact with the marketing team, website team, fundraising team, software engineer team, and HR.

The position of manager offers numerous benefits. The manager will not only observe but lead a real business, seeing the behind-the-scenes work of nonprofits. They will learn and use all of the modern platforms of small businesses such as Slack and Notion and meet with other managers to implement strategic plans to lead the organization forwards. Feel free to have a dream for the program and go for it! Being part of Bit by Bit provides a community of like-minded peers, people who can offer advice, encouragement, and support. Interacting with the wide range of stakeholders involved with Hello World will allow your communication skills to flourish. At the end of the year, the manager will have the satisfaction of knowing that they made an impact on a large group of middle school students.

Prerequisites

  • Comfortable coding in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

Key Skills

  • Organized
    • Able to keep track of emails, students, and volunteers
  • Good communication skills
    • Including teaching skills
  • Flexibility and adaptability to changes

Overseeing one of Bit by Bit's main programs is a large responsibility. You must be willing to put in around 3 to 5 hours a week during the spring and 5 to 10 hours during the summer. This position lasts until the end of summer 2022. We will be asking for a reference on the application, but this reference does not need to be too formal. We understand with current circumstances and by nature of age, not everyone will have previous work experience and we do not want that to stop anyone from applying. The reference can be a teacher, mentor, or anybody else who you believe will be able to speak to your abilities as a team player and leader, along with providing insight into your organizational skills and work ethic.

Applications will close March 19th.

Hello World Assistant Manager:

The Hello World Assistant Manager will work on the curriculum, scheduling, and assisting the manager. The position requires persistence, patience, and perseverance. They will be responsible for inspiring the next generation of computer programmers.

The assistant manager will update the curriculum to meet the needs of the program including making slides, worksheets, creating activities, and more. They will be responsible for helping volunteers learn and understand the curriculum and how to implement it. The assistant manager will also be a support for the manager of the program and will lead the program some days.

The assistant manager works closely with the software engineer team to design interactive tutorials and other things that will enhance the educational experience of the program.

The position of assistant manager offers numerous benefits. The assistant manager will not only observe but lead a real business, seeing the behind-the-scenes work of nonprofits.
